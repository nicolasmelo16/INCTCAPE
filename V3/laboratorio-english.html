Laboratory

INCT CAPE Multi-user Laboratory

The INCT CAPE multi-user laboratory features three infrastructures for research in control and automation of energy systems, all in the implementation phase. These include a plant for analyzing multiphase fluid flow, a plant for studies in solar thermal energy, and a microgrid with photovoltaic solar energy generation, wind energy emulation, and energy storage in batteries and green hydrogen. Equipment such as photovoltaic panels, lithium batteries, electrolyzers, fuel cells, thermal tanks, heat exchangers, separators, and others can be used for experiments, both individually and in combination. As a multidisciplinary and multi-user laboratory, experiments must be planned and scheduled in advance. This page provides the necessary procedures for users wishing to conduct experiments.

Laboratory Usage Rules

To use the laboratory, the user must contact INCT CAPE via email at inctenergia@contato.ufsc.br indicating the equipment to be used and the type of experiment. Instructions will be provided by email to formalize the request. Your request will be evaluated by the laboratory’s management committee, and if approved, the scheduling will be made.

Available Equipment

The list of available equipment is constantly being updated, as the INCT CAPE multi-user laboratory is still under development and purchases are made monthly. Please contact us to check current availability by emailing inctenergia@contato.ufsc.br.
