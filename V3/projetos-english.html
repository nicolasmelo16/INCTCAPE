Projects

Control and optimization of energy generation, usage and storage systems, with an emphasis on renewable energy

Description: This international research project aims to contribute to Brazil's scientific development and technological innovation in the fields of control and optimization. Specifically, it considers the application of innovative control techniques to energy generation, use, and storage systems, focusing on renewable sources. The main goal of this proposal is to increase the participation of Brazilian researchers in cooperation projects with international research groups and networks in the area of renewable energy systems, thereby promoting internationalization and scientific and technological development.
Funding: CNPq.
Start: January 2024

Multi-user Laboratory for Green Hydrogen Studies at the National Institute of Science and Technology in Control and Automation of Energy Processes

Description: This project focuses specifically on green hydrogen, from the generation process using renewable sources to its use as an energy storage element for transportation or to meet greater demands in power grids. The objective of this proposal is to complement INCT CAPE's laboratory infrastructure to enable interaction among UFSC research groups currently engaged in the green hydrogen topic, allowing them to carry out more complex projects in multidisciplinary areas involving the entire hydrogen cycle in energy systems.
Funding: FAPESC.
Start: January 2024

Advanced control and optimization for renewable energy systems with hydrogen storage

Description: Efficient energy generation and balanced consumption, as well as the use of renewable sources, are key factors for sustainable development. Within the renewable energy area, this project focuses on hydrogen (H2) production from renewable sources and the optimization of its use in microgrids. Using renewable energy to produce H2 enables projecting this fuel into a sustainable future.
Funding: CNPq.
Start: January 2022

Predictive control, delay compensation, and optimization: Theory and applications in the energy sector

Description: This project aims to research theoretical and applied aspects of predictive control algorithms, delay compensation, and optimization techniques, mainly targeting renewable and non-renewable energy production and management systems as well as microgrids.
Funding: CNPq.
Start: January 2020
